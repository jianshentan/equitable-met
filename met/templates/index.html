Why Does This Matter?

A flat admission fee of $25 cuts off the very visitors the met needs most: those on the fence about coming

The Met is not a universal space. Its location and collection reflect the values of its affluent, largely white donors. For visitors outside these demographics, the cost of entering the Met goes beyond admission. In entering the Met, they invest time and energy in a new space with no guarantee that what they find there will reflect, much less center, their lived experience.

To work towards its mission to “reach out to the widest possible audience in a spirit of inclusiveness” the Met depends on visitors who take a risk in stepping through the door.

Sliding scale admission offers a way to honor the non-monetary contributions made by low income visitors.

Where the Met Stands

The average ticket price paid by the Met’s ~4.5M visitors in 2017. The Met’s suggested price for an adult ticket is $25.
